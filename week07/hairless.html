Hairless Cats

main

Sphynx: A unique breed with a natural lack of fur, known for their distinctive appearance and affectionate personalities. Some have whiskers and eyebrows while others do not, but they all have charming wrinkles and long toes.


Bambino: A newcomer to the hairless cat scene, Bambinos were founded in 2005. Not yet accepted as an official breed by the Cat Fanciers Association—they’re considered an “experimental breed”—a Bambino cat is a cross of the hairless Sphynx and short-legged Munchkin.


Peterbald: This hairless cat breed is all ears—really, they have big perky ears that seem much too large for their wedge-shaped head. Peterbalds are rare outside of Russia, but friendly and full of energy. They like to play fetch and follow on their humans’ heels.
